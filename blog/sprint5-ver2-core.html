Programming Bootcamp

I am "all-in". This was my answer to my facilitators and fellow EDA students on our most recent Zoom Check-In Meeting. I truly mean what I said. I want to become a software developer after EDA's bootcamp. And here's my W-H-Y: Becoming a software developer is a life-long learning! I know when I chose this path that it will be very challenging, yet I also know that the reward of studying programming will immensely satisfy my hunger for learning for life.

I have gotten to know my main strength that could really help me in bootcamp. I am a good listener. This could really help me especially when the time comes that I need to work with a big team. But there's one thing I need to watch out for as well - too much listening is not going to be beneficial to the team. I also need to communicate to them my ideas in a respectful manner. On the other hand, I have been working on a solution to my main weakness, that is having a Learning Plan. So here's a short list of my committment:

  1. Attend ALL Zoom Meetings. Be On time.
  2. Participate in any group discussions. Learn how to give and receive feedbacks effectively.
  3. Practice all the coding materials from Monday to Friday and half-day of Saturday.
