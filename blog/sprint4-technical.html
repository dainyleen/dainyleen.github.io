How I Solve Problems

In my life, I have solved quite a few number of different kinds of problems. My top 2 ways of solving a problem are: Researching/Googling and Reflecting.

Scenario One

I love playing Sims4 game. There was this one time, I got bored with playing my sims with only a small amount of Simoleons (money in the real world) over and over again. I wanted my sim to experience living in the mansion which was unoccupied. What did I do? I solved the problem by using "googling" this question in my head, "how to acquire lots of simoleons?" It felt so easy because there was this sims4 community already, who were using the "code" to get more Simoleons. I find Google extremely helpful in solving simple problems.

Scenario Two

When I received a call from my facilitator that I would not be moving on to bootcamp last year, I took it as a big problem! I felt like I wasted time and money just because of me.

I faced this problem by "reflecting" on ways I could improve on in terms of my time management and my ability to focus. I waited for 4 weeks so I can enrol again into another batch of aspiring programmers. While waiting, I subscibed to CodeAcademy and finished the basic html and css courses. I find personal reflection beneficial in solving internal problems.
